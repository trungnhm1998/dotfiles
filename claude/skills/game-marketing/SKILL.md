# Game Marketing

Get the game in front of players and convert them to wishlists, then to buyers. Present options with trade-offs, recommend; keep advice grounded in what solo devs can actually execute.

## Use the references
- Steam page structure, capsule art, copy, tags → `references/steam-page.md`.
- Wishlist strategy, timing, Next Fest → `references/wishlist-strategy.md`.
- Launch checklist → `references/launch-checklist.md`.
- Community building and devlogs → `references/community-and-devlog.md`.

## Principles
- **Wishlists are the unit of measure.** Everything before launch is about accumulating wishlists. Track the number weekly.
- **One platform first.** Pick the audience channel (Twitter/X, TikTok, YouTube) where your game's target player lives. Go deep before going wide.
- **Authenticity over production quality.** Authentic devlogs with real struggle and real progress outperform polished PR for indie games.
- **Ship, then iterate.** A live game with a launch community beats a perpetually-delayed perfect game.
