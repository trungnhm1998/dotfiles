# Wishlist Strategy

## Why Wishlists Matter

Wishlists directly drive Steam's launch day algorithm:
- Steam sends a launch notification to everyone who wishlisted the game.
- The launch day spike in sales determines where you appear on New Releases, Popular New Releases, and recommendation widgets.
- A higher wishlist count → bigger day-1 spike → better algorithmic placement → more organic sales.

**Benchmark (rough, not official):** 7 000+ wishlists at launch correlates with breaking even for a modest solo-dev game. 1 000–3 000 is common for first games without marketing. Track your velocity weekly.

## Wishlist Conversion Triggers

What actually drives people to wishlist:

1. **Trailers and GIFs** — motion content converts. Short gameplay GIFs on social outperform screenshots.
2. **Demo availability** — a free demo on Steam dramatically increases wishlists (especially during Next Fest).
3. **Press / content creators** — a YouTube video or Twitch stream from a relevant creator can send hundreds of wishlists in hours.
4. **Social presence** — consistent posting on the platform where your target players are (not just generic social media).
5. **Reddit** (r/indiegaming, r/gamedev, r/[genre subreddits]) — Show HN style posts about the game. Rule: lead with genuine content, not just a sales link.
6. **Events** — Steam Next Fest, PAX Online, itch.io game jams (raise awareness, then funnel to Steam page).

## Next Fest

Steam Next Fest (formerly "Steam Game Festival") runs 3 times a year (typically February, June, October).

**Requirements:** Your game must have a free demo on Steam; the game must not yet be released.

**Why participate:** Next Fest can deliver 1 000–10 000+ wishlists in one week. Visibility is algorithm-driven (trailers, demo downloads). Coordinate a trailer, social push, and streamer outreach to maximize the window.

**How to apply:** Steamworks → App Admin → Demos → submit a demo build; then register for Next Fest through the Steamworks event page (registration opens weeks before).

## Wishlist Page Timing

| Phase | Action |
|---|---|
| 6–12 months before launch | Open Steam page with capsule + screenshots. Announce on social. |
| 3–6 months before launch | Consistent social presence; first trailer; devlog cadence. |
| Next Fest window (if applicable) | Participate with a demo. Major wishlist push. |
| 1–2 months before launch | Second trailer (launch trailer); ramp social frequency. |
| Launch week | Email all wishlists via Valve's system (automatic on launch). Manual outreach to press/streamers. |

## Tracking

- **Steamworks → Marketing → Wishlist** shows cumulative and daily adds/removes.
- Watch for drops after key events (trailer, Next Fest) — drop rate reveals whether the audience is right.
- Track which posts/events correlate with spikes using date annotations.

## Realistic Expectations (solo dev, first game)

- 500–2 000 wishlists is common without prior audience or marketing spend.
- 2 000–7 000 requires consistent social presence + at least one viral moment or press pickup.
- 10 000+ usually requires: Next Fest participation, press coverage, or a creator with a large audience.

Do not delay launch indefinitely waiting for a "big enough" wishlist count. Launch, gather data, improve.
